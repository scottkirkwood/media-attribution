In the US, following legislative changes in 1988, all works (whether they have a copyright notice or not) are automatically copyrighted and cannot be used without the author's permission. For instance, if you find a shopping list on the floor, you legally cannot publish it without the consent of the 'author'.

The Creative Commons licenses use the copyright laws to permit other people to use the work, with some restrictions. The most common restriction is that you should attribute the work to the original author.

The Creative Commons licenses do not require you to inform a creator that you are using her CC-licensed work, but it's a nice thing to do. Most people are very happy to learn that someone is using and building upon their creations; that's why they use Creative Commons licenses in the first place.
